# Scoring Rubric

Adapted from [hardikpandya/stop-slop](https://github.com/hardikpandya/stop-slop), MIT.

After rewriting, score the prose 1–10 on each dimension. Combined score below 35/50 means revise again.

| Dimension | Question | 10 = | 1 = |
|-----------|----------|------|------|
| **Directness** | Statements or announcements? | "Our error budget is 0.1%, and we burned 80% of it in February." | "It's important to note that we have a robust error budget framework which we strive to maintain rigorously." |
| **Rhythm** | Varied or metronomic? | Sentences mix 4 words and 25 words. Paragraphs end in different shapes. | Every sentence is 12–18 words. Every paragraph ends with a punchline. |
| **Trust** | Respects reader intelligence? | Leaves the conclusion implicit when the evidence makes it obvious. | "What this means is..." "Let me explain..." "In other words..." |
| **Authenticity** | Sounds human? | Has friction. Names dates, places, frustrations. Uses contractions. | Polished. Generic. Could be anyone. No specifics. |
| **Density** | Anything cuttable? | Every word load-bearing. Removing one would lose meaning. | Could lose 30% of words without losing meaning. |

## Quick checks before delivering

- Any adverbs? Kill them.
- Any passive voice? Find the actor, make them the subject.
- Inanimate thing doing a human verb? Name the person.
- Sentence starts with a Wh- word? Restructure.
- Any "here's what/this/that" throat-clearing? Cut to the point.
- Any "not X, it's Y" contrasts? State Y directly.
- Three consecutive sentences match length? Break one.
- Paragraph ends with punchy one-liner? Vary it.
- Em-dash anywhere? Remove it.
- Vague declarative ("The implications are significant")? Name the specific implication.
- Narrator-from-a-distance ("Nobody designed this")? Put the reader in the scene.
- Meta-joiners ("The rest of this essay...")? Delete. Let the essay move.
